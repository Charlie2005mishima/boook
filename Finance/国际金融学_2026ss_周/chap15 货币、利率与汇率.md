## 货币的定义
Narrow Definition : Currency in circulation, checking deposits, and debit card accounts
Broader Definition : Deposits of currency (excluded from narrow definition)
liquid: repay debt without substantial transaction costs
illiquid asset: substantial transaction costs in terms of time, effort, fees
交换媒介、计价单位、价值贮藏
货币供给：联邦储备系统所称的货币总量 M1

## money supply
The central bank substantially controls the quantity of money that circulates in an economy--the money supply.

## 个人货币需求
1. 预期收益 interest rates difference: opportunity cost, the interest rate on non-monetary assets
2. 风险: risk: unexpected inflation
3. 流动性: liquidity: the price of transactions the quantity of goods

## 货币总需求
1. 利率：higer interest rate >> higher opportunity cost >> lower demand of money
2. 价格水平：higher average price >> larger need for liquidity >> higher demand
3. 实际国民收入 real income：higher real GNP >> more goods in transacrions >> larger need for liquidity >> higher demand

## 长期货币、价格水平和汇率

- short run: prices do not have sufficient time to adjust to market conditions.
- long run: prices of factors of production and of output have sufficient time to adjust to market conditions.
	- 长期均衡：向充分就业状态的调整过程，如果不出现新的经济震荡，经济最终可以达到的状态
	- The expectation on future exchange rate depends on the long-run exchange rate.
	- Factors that determine long-run exchange rate
		- monetary factors
		- relative output changes
### 货币和货币价格
货币商场均衡条件：$M^s/P=L(R,Y)$ 
inflation rate: $\Delta P/P=\Delta M^s/M^s-\Delta L/L$ 
货币供给的永久性增加，将使价格水平的长期值相应地增加。
尤其是经济最初处于充分就业状态时，货币供给的永久性增加，最终将使价格水平同比例上升。

### 短期价格刚性和长期价格灵活性

### 永久性货币供给变动和汇率
- How does a permanent increase of the money supply cause prices and wages to change?
	- Excess demand of goods and services: a higher quantity of money supplied implies that people have more funds available to pay for goods and services.
	- inflationary expectation
- When we consider price changes in the long run, inflationary expectations will have an effect in foreign exchange markets.

### 汇率超调 !
- 汇率超调 exchange rate overshooting：汇率对于货币波动的即刻反应超过了长期反应
	- predicted to occur when monetary policy has an immediate effect on interest rates, but not on prices.
	- A permanent increase in a country's money supply causes a proportional long run depreciation of its currency.
