## chap1 preferences and utility
### 1.1 basic setting 
**※【定义】** future state $\mathbf{e}=\{ e_{1},\dots,e_N \}$

**※【定义】** probability of vector $P=\{ p_{1},\dots,p_{N} \}$

**※【定义】** preference $P^1\succ P^2,\,P^1\sim P^2,\,P^1\prec P^2,\,P^1\preceq P^2$

**※【公理】** complete axiom
> for any two vectors of preference $P^1,P^{2}$, one and only one of the following three statements is true $P^1\succ P^2,\,P^1\sim P^2,\,P^1\prec P^2$

**※【公理】** transitivity axiom
> for any three lotterries $P,P^1,P^2$, $P^1\succeq P,\,P\succeq P^2\implies P^1\succeq P^2$

**※【定义】** compound lottery $P=\lambda P^1+(1-\lambda)P^2,\,\lambda\in[0,1]$

**※【公理】** continuity axiom
> $P,P^1,P^2$, suppose $P^1\succeq P,\,P\succeq P^{2}$ then there exists a $\lambda\in[0,1],\text{ s.t. }P\sim\lambda P^1+(1-\lambda)P^{2}$

**※【公理】** independence axiom
> $P^1\succ P^2\Leftrightarrow\lambda P^1+(1-\lambda)P\succ\lambda P^{2}+(1-\lambda)P,\,\forall\,\lambda\in[0,1]$
> $P^1\sim P^2\Leftrightarrow\lambda P^1+(1-\lambda)P\sim\lambda P^{2}+(1-\lambda)P,\,\forall\,\lambda\in[0,1]$

### 1.3 utility function
**※【定义】** expected utility
> $U(P)=E[u(\mathbf{e})]=\sum_{i=1}^Np_{i}u(e_{i}),\,U(P^1)\geq U(P^{2})\Leftrightarrow P^1\geq P^{2}$

**※【定义】** fundamental lotteries
> $P^{(i)}=\{ 0,\dots,1,\dots,0 \}$

rearrange: $P^{(1)}\succeq P^{(2)}\succeq\dots\succeq P^{(N)}$
$\implies\forall\,i\in[1,2,\dots,N],\,P^{(i)}\sim\lambda_{i}P^{(1)}+(1-\lambda_{i})P^{(N)}$
$\implies \begin{cases}P=\{ p_{1},p_{2},\dots,p_{N-1},p_{N} \}\sim P^*=\left\{ \sum_{i=1}^Np_{i}\lambda_{i},0,\dots,0,\sum_{i=1}^Np_{i}(1-\lambda_{i}) \right\}\\Q=\{ q_{1},q_{2},\dots,q_{N-1},q_{N} \}\sim q^*=\left\{ \sum_{i=1}^Nq_{i}\lambda_{i},0,\dots,0,\sum_{i=1}^Nq_{i}(1-\lambda_{i})\right\}\end{cases}$
$\implies \begin{cases}P^*\succ Q^*\Leftrightarrow \sum_{i=1}^Np_{i}\lambda_{i}>\sum_{i=1}^Nq_{i}\lambda_{i}\\P^*\sim Q^*\Leftrightarrow \sum_{i=1}^Np_{i}\lambda_{i}=\sum_{i=1}^Nq_{i}\lambda_{i}\end{cases}$

### 1.3 risk aversion
**※【定义】** absolute risk aversion $R_{a}(W)=-\frac{u''(W)}{u'(W)}$

**※【定义】** CARA utility constant absolute risk aversion
> $u(W)=1-\exp(-\gamma W),\,\gamma>0$ ($\gamma$: constant absolute risk aversion)

**※【定义】** relative risk aversion $R_{r}(W)=-\frac{Wu''(W)}{u'(W)}$

**※【定义】** CRRA utility constant relative risk aversion
> $u(W)=\begin{cases}\frac{W^{1-\gamma}}{1-\gamma}&\gamma>0\\ \ln W&\gamma=1\end{cases}$  ($\gamma$: constant relative risk aversion)

## chap3 linear factor model

### 1. CAPM 的推导基础

均值-方差有效的前提条件：investors choose mean-variance efficient portfolios
e.g. 
二次效用函数：$U = \mathrm{E}\left[-c(W_T - a)^2\right]$
多元正态分布：$f_R(r) = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^{\frac{N}{2}}|\Sigma|^{\frac{1}{2}}} e^{-\frac{(r - \mu)^T\Sigma^{-1}(r - \mu)}{2}}$
结论：
- 在均值-方差有效且存在无风险资产的条件下，市场组合即为无风险资产和切点资产组合。
- 所有投资者均持有 mean-variance efficient 组合，所以总体的持有也是 mean-variance efficient 。
### 2. CAPM 核心公式
期望收益关系：$\mathrm{E}[R_i] = R_f + \beta_i (\mathrm{E}[R_M] - R_f)$
其中：$\beta_i = \frac{\mathrm{Cov}[R_i, R_M]}{\mathrm{Var}[R_M]}$
收益分解：$R_i = R_f + \beta_i (R_M - R_f) + \epsilon_i$
其中：$\mathrm{E}[\epsilon_i] = 0, \quad \mathrm{Cov}[\epsilon_i, R_M] = 0$ 
### 3. 单因子模型的一般理论 

#### 对任意均值-方差最优组合 R_{MVO}：
$\mathrm{E}[R_i] = a_{MVO} + b_{MVO} \beta_i, \quad \beta_i = \frac{\mathrm{Cov}[R_i, R_{MVO}]}{\mathrm{Var}[R_{MVO}]}$
- 若存在无风险资产：$a_{MVO} = R_f,\ b_{MVO} = \mathrm{E}[R_{MVO}] - R_f$
- 若无无风险资产：$a_{MVO} = c,\ b_{MVO} = \mathrm{E}[R_{MVO}] - c$，$c$为常数
#### 单因子模型成立的条件（交易因子）：
- 因子价格为 1：因子本身必须是均值-方差最优组合（非无风险资产）
- 因子价格非 0 非 1：因子是某个均值-方差最优组合的倍数
- 因子价格为 0：因子是两个均值-方差最优组合的差
### 4. 非交易因子与最大相关组合（第29–32页）
#### 因子分解：
$F = c + \Gamma^T R + \eta, \quad \mathrm{E}[\eta] = 0,\ \mathrm{Cov}[R, \eta] = 0$
其中 $F^* = \Gamma^T R$是与 $F$\最大相关的组合。
#### 模型等价性：
$\beta_{i,F} = \beta_{i,F^*} \cdot \frac{\mathrm{Var}[F^*]}{\mathrm{Var}[F^*] + \mathrm{Var}[\eta]}$
$a_F = a_{F^*}, \quad b_F = b_{F^*} \left(1 + \frac{\mathrm{Var}[\eta]}{\mathrm{Var}[F^*]}\right)$

### 5. 消费CAPM（CCAPM）
#### 两期优化问题：
假设时间可分离效用：$u(c_0, c_1) = u(c_0) + \delta u(c_1)$
#### 一阶条件（定价核）：
$P_0 = \mathrm{E}\left[P_1 \delta \frac{u'(c_1^*)}{u'(c_0^*)}\right]$
#### 用收益率表示：
$1 = \mathrm{E}\left[R_{0,1} \delta \frac{u'(c_1^*)}{u'(c_0^*)}\right]$
#### 单因子形式：
$\mathrm{E}[R_{0,1}] = \frac{1}{\mathrm{E}[m]} + \beta \left(-\frac{\mathrm{Var}[m]}{\mathrm{E}[m]}\right)$
其中：$m = \delta \frac{u'(c_1^*)}{u'(c_0^*)}, \quad \beta = \frac{\mathrm{Cov}[R_{0,1}, m]}{\mathrm{Var}[m]}$
### 6. 多因子模型与单因子模型的等价性

#### 多因子模型形式：
\[
$\mathrm{E}[R_i] = R_f + \beta_i^T \Lambda$
\]
其中：
\[
$\beta_i = \Sigma_{FF}^{-1} \Sigma_{FR}, \quad \Sigma_{FF} = \mathrm{Var}[F], \quad \Sigma_{FR} = \mathrm{Cov}[F, R_i]$
\]

#### 等价单因子构造：
取：
\[
$k = \Sigma_{FF}^{-1} \Lambda, \quad \lambda^* = \Lambda^T \Sigma_{FF}^{-1} \Lambda$
\]
则单因子 \(F^* = k^T F\) 给出的预期收益与多因子模型完全相同。

### 7. 定价核与因子冗余性（第54–62页）

#### 定价核形式：
\[
$m = a + b^T (F - \mathrm{E}[F]), \quad a = \frac{1}{R_f}$
\]
满足：
\[
$\mathrm{E}[R_i m] = 1$

#### 因子冗余的判断：

- 正确方法：检验 \(b\) 对应元素是否为 0

### 8. APT（套利定价理论）（第63–68页）

#### 单因子举例（无特质风险）：
\[
$R_i = a_i + b_i f, \quad \mathrm{E}[f] = 0$
\]
无套利条件：
\[
$\frac{a_i - r_f}{b_i} = \lambda \quad (\text{对所有 } i)$
\]

#### 多因子APT形式：
\[
$\mathrm{E}[R_i] = \lambda_0 + \sum_{j=1}^k b_{ij} \lambda_j + \nu_i$
\]
其中 \(\nu_i\) 为偏离项，满足：
\[
$\sum_{i=1}^n \nu_i = 0, \quad \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n \nu_i^2 = 0, \quad \sum_{i=1}^n b_{ij} \nu_i = 0$
\]

#### APT结论：
- 当资产数量相对于因子数量足够大时，预期收益近似满足线性多因子模型
- 若存在无风险资产，则 \(\lambda_0 \approx R_f\)

## chap4 state price, arbitrage, market completeness
### 4.1 stata price
price vector $\mathbf{P}=\begin{bmatrix}P_{1} \\ \vdots \\ P_{M}\end{bmatrix}$ 
payoffs matrix $\mathbf{X}=\begin{bmatrix}X_{1} & \cdots & X_{1N} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ X_{M1} & \cdots & X_{MN}\end{bmatrix}$  
state price vector $\phi = \left[ \begin{array}{l} \phi_ {1} \\ \vdots \\ \phi_ {N} \end{array} \right]$ s.t. $P=X\phi$ 
 investment strategies $\psi=\begin{bmatrix}\psi_{1} \\ \vdots \\ \psi_{M}\end{bmatrix}$

### 4.2 law of one price

**※【定义】** loop
If two investment strategies $\psi_{1}$ and $\psi_{2}$ have the same payos $\boldsymbol {\psi} _ {1} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {X} = \boldsymbol {\psi} _ {2} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {X},$ then they also have the same price $\boldsymbol {\psi} _ {1} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {P} = \boldsymbol {\psi} _ {2} ^ {T} \boldsymbol {P}.$ 

**※【定理】** loop & state price
The Law Of One Price is satised if and only if a state price vector **exists**.

**※【定理】** no redundant
if the matrix X has rank M, the only way two investment strategies can have the same payo, is to be the same.
(then loop is trivial; and we can choose state prices to be$\boldsymbol {\phi} = \boldsymbol {X} ^ {T} \left(\boldsymbol {X} \boldsymbol {X} ^ {T}\right) ^ {- 1} \boldsymbol {P}$ )
(when redundant, after excluding the redundant asset, $\boldsymbol {\phi} = \left(\boldsymbol {X} ^ {*}\right) ^ {T} \left[ \boldsymbol {X} ^ {*} \left(\boldsymbol {X} ^ {*}\right) ^ {T} \right] ^ {- 1} \boldsymbol {P} ^ {*}$ )
(slides 还论证了如果有redundant的也可以处理)

### 4.3 arbitrage

**※【定义】** arbitrage
1 The current price is zero or negative.   
2 The future cash ows are never negative.   
3 The future cash ow is positive with at least some probability.
or
1 The current price is negative.   
2 The future cash ows are never negative.

**※【定义】** arbitrage in terms of 
either $\psi^ {T} X \geq 0 \quad \text {a n d} \quad \psi^ {T} P <   0,$ or $\psi^ {T} X > 0 \quad \text {and} \quad \psi^ {T} P \leq 0$ 

**※【定理】** arbitrage and state price
There are no arbitrage opportunities if and only if there exists a **strictly positive** vector of state prices.

### 4.4 market completeness

**※【定义】** market complete
Markets are complete if, for any arbitrary $1 \times N$ payo vector Z , there is a strategy $\psi$ such that $\psi ^ { T } X = Z$ .

**※【定理】** market complete and loop Second Fundamental Theorem of Asset Pricing
If the Law Of One Price holds, then markets are complete if and only if there exists a unique state price vector.

### 4.5 pricing kernel

**※【定义】** pricing kernel
A pricing kernel or stochastic discount factor is a random variable m with the property that $P = \operatorname {E} [ m X ]$ for any asset or portfolio, where we have treated the payos X as a random variable. $P = \operatorname {E} [ m X ] = \sum_ {i = 1} ^ {N} p _ {i} m _ {i} X _ {i}$

**※【定理】** state price and stochastic discount factor $m _ {i} = \frac {\phi_ {i}}{p _ {i}}$

**※【定理】** 
1 A stochastic discount factor exists if and only if the Law Of One Price is satised.   
2 A strictly positive stochastic discount factor exists if and only of there are no arbitrage opportunities.   
3 Provided the Law Of One Price is satised, the stochastic discount factor is unique if and only if markets are complete.

**※【定理】** stochastic discount factor and risk-free  $\mathsf {E} [ m ] = \frac {1}{R _ {f}}$

### 4.6 relation to linear factor models

**※【定理】** stochastic discount factor model for asset prices
$\begin{array}{l} E [ R ] = R _ {f} - R _ {f} \operatorname {C o v} [ m, R ] \\ = R _ {f} + \frac {\operatorname {C o v} [ m , R ]}{\operatorname {V a r} [ m ]} (- R _ {f} \operatorname {V a r} [ m ]) \\ = R _ {f} + \beta (- R _ {f} \operatorname {V a r} [ m ]) \\ \end{array}$   where $\beta \equiv \frac {\operatorname {C o v} [ m , R ]}{\operatorname {V a r} [ m ]}.$ 
 
Suppose CAPM is true. $\mathsf {E} \left[ R \right] = R _ {f} + \beta \left(\mathsf {E} \left[ R _ {M} \right] - R _ {f}\right), \quad \beta \equiv \frac {\mathsf {C o v} \left[ R , R _ {M} \right]}{\mathsf {V a r} \left[ R _ {M} \right]}.$ 
Suppose stochastic discount factor of form $m = a + b R _ {M}.$ 
Then $\begin{array}{l} 1 = \operatorname {E} [ R m ] = \operatorname {E} [ R (a + b R _ {M}) ] = \operatorname {E} [ R ] (a + b \operatorname {E} [ R _ {M} ]) + b \operatorname {C o v} [ R, R _ {M} ] \\ = (a + b \operatorname {E} [ R _ {M} ]) [ R _ {f} + \beta (\operatorname {E} [ R _ {M} ] - R _ {f}) ] + b \operatorname {C o v} [ R, R _ {M} ] \\ = (a + b \operatorname {E} [ R _ {M} ]) \left[ R _ {f} + \frac {\operatorname {C o v} [ R , R _ {M} ]}{\operatorname {V a r} [ R _ {M} ]} (\operatorname {E} [ R _ {M} ] - R _ {f}) \right] + b \operatorname {C o v} [ R, R _ {M} ]. \\ \end{array}$ 
$a = \frac {\operatorname {E} \left[ R _ {M} \left(R _ {M} - R _ {f}\right) \right]}{R _ {f} \operatorname {V a r} \left[ R _ {M} \right]} \quad \text {a n d} \quad b = \frac {R _ {f} - \operatorname {E} \left[ R _ {M} \right]}{R _ {f} \operatorname {V a r} \left[ R _ {M} \right]}$

### 5.1 recap of linear factor models

 For any mean-variance optimal portfolio (except the global minimum variance portfolio) with return $R_{mvo}$, we have $\operatorname {E} \left[ R _ {i} \right] = a _ {m v o} + b _ {m v o} \beta_ {i}$, where $\beta _{i}=Cov[R_{i},R_{mvo}]/Var[R_{mvo}]$
For CAMP, $\mathrm {E} [ R _ {i} ] = R _ {f} + \beta_ {i} (\mathrm {E} [ R _ {M} ] - R _ {f}),\,\mathrm {where }\, \beta_ {i} = \operatorname {C o v} \left[ R _ {i}, R _ {M} \right] / \operatorname {Var} \left[ R _ {M} \right]$

### 5.5 empirical tests of linear factor models

to evaluate the following model: $\mathsf {E} \left[ R _ {i} ^ {e} \right] = \lambda_ {1} \beta_ {i 1} + \dots + \lambda_ {K} \beta_ {i K}$ 
with $R _ { i } ^ { e }$ indicates excess return over $r_{f}$   
the betas are defined by regression coefficients from $R _ {i t} ^ {e} = \alpha_ {i} + \beta_ {i 1} f _ {1 t} + \dots + \beta_ {i K} f _ {K t} + \varepsilon_ {i t}$

Types of tests Time-series regression test Cross-sectional regression test

- Time-series test

Important assumption: the factors are tradable.
For any factor $f _ { j }$ , let us estimate $f _ {j t} = \alpha_ {j} + \beta_ {j 1} f _ {1 t} + \dots + \beta_ {j j} f _ {j t} + \dots + \beta_ {j K} f _ {K t} + \varepsilon_ {j t}$ 
 The coefficient estimates will be $\beta_ {j j} = 1, a n d\, \alpha_ {j} = \beta_ {j 1} = \dots = \beta_ {j K} = 0$ 
 
 Steps of estimation and evaluation
1 Run time-series regressions for each asset $R _ {i t} ^ {e} = \alpha_ {i} + \beta_ {i 1} f _ {1 t} + \dots + \beta_ {i K} f _ {K t} + \varepsilon_ {i t}$
2 The estimate of the factor risk premiums are just the sample means of the factors $\hat {\lambda} _ {j} = \hat {E} [ f _ {j t} ]$ 
3 Use t-tests to check whether the individual pricing errors αi are zero $\mathsf {H} _ {0}: \alpha_ {i} = 0$
4 See the next slide on how to test whether all the pricing errors are jointly zero $\mathsf {H} _ {0}: \alpha_ {1} = \alpha_ {2} = \ldots = \alpha_ {N} = 0$

Testing whether all the pricing errors are jointly zero
• Assuming normal and i.i.d errors, the following test statistics can be used $\frac {T - N - K}{N} (1 + \hat {E} [ \mathrm {f} ] ^ {\prime} \widehat {\Omega} ^ {- 1} \hat {E} [ f ]) ^ {- 1} \hat {\alpha} ^ {\prime} \widehat {\Sigma} ^ {- 1} \hat {\alpha} \sim F _ {N, T - N - K}$
where
• N - number of assets (must be much smaller than T),   
• K - number of factors,   
• Ω - variance-covariance matrix of the factors, and   $\widehat {\Omega} = \frac {1}{T} \sum_ {t = 1} ^ {T} \left[ f _ {t} - \widehat {E} [ f ] \right] \left[ f _ {t} - \widehat {E} [ f ] \right] ^ {\prime}$ 
• ∑ - variance-covariance matrix of the regression residuals. $\widehat {\boldsymbol {\Sigma}} = \frac {1}{T} \sum_ {t = 1} ^ {T} \hat {\varepsilon} _ {t} \hat {\varepsilon} ^ {\prime} _ {t}$ 
• If there is only one factor (K = 1), as in the case of the CAPM, then $\frac {T - N - K}{N} (1 + (\frac {\hat {E} [ f ]}{\hat {\sigma} (f)}) ^ {2}) ^ {- 1} \hat {\alpha} ^ {\prime} \widehat {\Sigma} ^ {- 1} \hat {\alpha} \sim F _ {N, T - N - 1}$

- Cross-sectional test

• We do not need the assumption that the factor is tradable.   
• Again, the model we are evaluating is in (1) $\mathsf {E} \left[ R _ {i} ^ {e} \right] = \lambda_ {1} \beta_ {i 1} + \dots + \lambda_ {K} \beta_ {i K}$

Steps of estimation and evaluation
1 Run time-series regressions for each asset to get betas $R _ {i t} ^ {e} = a _ {i} + \beta_ {i 1} f _ {1 t} + \dots + \beta_ {i K} f _ {K t} + \varepsilon_ {i t}$
2 Estimate the following cross-sectional regression of average returns on betas $\hat {E} \left[ R _ {i} ^ {e} \right] = \lambda_ {1} \hat {\beta} _ {i 1} + \dots + \lambda_ {K} \hat {\beta} _ {i K} + \alpha_ {i}$ 
3 For inference, see the following slides.

Inference
• Let $\hat { \beta }$ denote the N (assets) × K (factors) matrix of estimated betas and the K × 1 vector of estimated lambdas:
$\hat {\beta} = \left[ \begin{array}{l l l} \hat {\beta} _ {1 1} & \dots & \hat {\beta} _ {1 K} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \hat {\beta} _ {N 1} & \dots & \hat {\beta} _ {N K} \end{array} \right] \quad \hat {\lambda} = \left[ \begin{array}{l} \hat {\lambda} _ {1} \\ \hat {\lambda} _ {2} \\ \dots \\ \hat {\lambda} _ {K} \end{array} \right]$

$V a r \big (\hat {\lambda} \big) = \frac {1}{T} [ (\hat {\beta} ^ {\prime} \hat {\beta}) ^ {- 1} \hat {\beta} ^ {\prime} \widehat {\sum} \hat {\beta} (\hat {\beta} ^ {\prime} \hat {\beta}) ^ {- 1} + \hat {\Omega} ]$
$V a r (\hat {\alpha}) = \frac {1}{T} [ I _ {N} - \hat {\beta} (\hat {\beta} ^ {\prime} \hat {\beta}) ^ {- 1} \hat {\beta} ^ {\prime} ] \widehat {\Sigma} [ I _ {N} - \hat {\beta} (\hat {\beta} ^ {\prime} \hat {\beta}) ^ {- 1} \hat {\beta} ^ {\prime} ] ^ {\prime}$ 

• To test if is statistically different from zero, we can use a simple t-test with the test statistic: $t = \frac {\hat {\lambda} _ {j}}{\sqrt {V a r (\hat {\lambda}) _ {j j}}}$ 

• We can test whether all the pricing errors are jointly zero with the test statistic: $\hat {\alpha} ^ {\prime} \operatorname {V a r} (\hat {\alpha}) ^ {- 1} \hat {\alpha} \sim \chi_ {N - K} ^ {2}$ 

Fama-French model

• The multi-factor model with the market $( R _ { M t } ^ { e } )$ , size (SMBt), and value (HMLt) factors is called the Fama-French model 
$\mathsf {E} \big [ R _ {i} ^ {e} \big ] = \beta_ {i M} \mathsf {E} [ R _ {M} ^ {e} ] + \beta_ {i S M B} \mathsf {E} [ S M B ] + \beta_ {i H M L} \mathsf {E} [ H M L ]$ 
where the βi estimates are calculated from the regressio $R _ {i t} ^ {e} = \alpha_ {i} + \beta_ {i M} R _ {M t} ^ {e} + \beta_ {i S M B} S M B _ {t} + \beta_ {i H M L} H M L _ {t} + u _ {i t}$
• The SMB and HML factors as defined by Fama and French are constructed as follows.   
• At the end of June each year sort all stocks based on their ME and BE/ME (from the previous year) to create 6 portfolios according to:
• Independent sorting. Cutoff points are determined based on NYSE stock only Yearly rebalancing, value-weighted portfolios.   
• The SMB and HML factors for the following year (beginning of July to end of June) are the monthly returns on two portfolios:
$S M B _ {t} = \frac {1}{3} (R _ {t, S G} + R _ {t, S N} + R _ {t, S V}) - \frac {1}{3} (R _ {t, B G} + R _ {t, B N} + R _ {t, B V})$
$H M L _ {t} = \frac {1}{2} (R _ {t, S V} + R _ {t, B V}) - \frac {1}{2} (R _ {t, S G} + R _ {t, B G})$ 

Carhart model

• The market, size, value, and momentum factors together form the Carhart (1997) model: $\mathsf {E} \big [ R _ {i} ^ {e} \big ] = \beta_ {i M} \mathsf {E} [ R _ {M} ^ {e} ] + \beta_ {i S M B} \mathsf {E} [ S M B ] + \beta_ {i H M L} \mathsf {E} [ H M L ] + \beta_ {i W M L} \mathsf {E} [ W M L ]$ where the βi estimates are calculated from the regression $R _ {i t} ^ {e} = \alpha_ {i} + \beta_ {i M} R _ {M t} ^ {e} + \beta_ {i S M B} S M B _ {t} + \beta_ {i H M L} H M L _ {t} + \beta_ {i W M L} W M L _ {t} + u _ {i t}$
• This is the most important benchmark in the cross-sectional asset pricing literature. 
$W H L _ {t} = \frac {1}{2} (R _ {t, S W} + R _ {t, B W}) - \frac {1}{2} (R _ {t, S L} + R _ {t, B L})$

- Banz (1981): small firms earn higher average returns, especially in January
	- Small firms are riskier than large firms? high leverage, low production efficiency
	- Small firms have other characteristics: Neglected firms: lower analyst coverage; High transaction costs: lower liquidity

### IVOL

Among overpriced (underpriced) stocks, the higher IVOL stocks are even more overpriced (underpriced) → negative (positive) future return

	- Investors overreact to past asset growth (expectation error)
