

# Neglective Inattention—a toy model

Assume $x ^ { i }$ is the stock demand of investor i, i= r or l. The utility is therefore: $x ^ {i} \left[ \mathrm {E} ^ {i} (d) \mathbf {- p} \right] - 0. 5 \gamma \mathrm {V a r} (x ^ {i} d).$ 

First‐order condition gives the optimal demand function as $x ^ {i} = [ \operatorname {E} ^ {i} (d) - p ] / [ A \operatorname {v a r} (d) ].$ 

Equating aggregate demand to aggregate supply (which is assumed to be zero) gives:

$$
m \left[ \mathrm {E} ^ {l} (d) - p \right] + (1 - m) \left[ \mathrm {E} ^ {r} (d) - p \right] = 0 \Rightarrow
$$

$$
p = m \mathrm {E} ^ {l} (d) + (1 - m) \mathrm {E} ^ {r} (d)
$$

In other words, the equilibrium price today is the weighted average of investors’ cash flow expectations.
